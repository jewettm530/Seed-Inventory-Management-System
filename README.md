# Seed-Inventory-Management-System
## Short Description 
A Garden Seed Inventory Management System is designed to help gardeners, farmers, and anyone else who enjoys growing plants from scratch to efficiently track and organize their seed collections. It allows users to catalog seeds by type, variety, purchase date, common name, recommended planting date, etc. Ideal for home gardeners, community gardens, and agricultural businesses, this system streamlines seed management for better planning and making it easier for you to know when your seeds are no longer good for planting. 

## Project Purpose 
To increase the ease and efficiency of organizing, tracking, managing and planning collections of seeds for anyone who plants gardens whether recreationally or as an agricultural business. 

## Anticipated Primary Audience 
Backyard gardeners, farmers, agricultural businesses, community gardens 

## How/Why A Garden Seed Management System Would Be Useful 
Allowing users to organize, track, and manage information for their seeds will ensure easy access to essential information, both helping users to make better decisions in how to plant their seeds and to prevent shortages in seed inventory or unnecessary seed purchases. This tool saves time, improves garden planning, enhances productivity, and promotes better seed-saving practices.  

## What the Project Will Provide 
The project will provide the ability to search and filter for seeds based on information such as type, variety, purchase date, common name, recommended planting date, etc. Users are able to input the date of purchase and see the expected expiration date of the seeds, as well as view the standard recommended planting dates based on the growing zone they provide.

## How to Run
1. Open your Oracle SQL Developer or APEX environment.
2. Run the scripts in the following order to handle dependencies:
  /scripts/create_tables.sql
  /scripts/insert_data.sql
  /scripts/views.sql
  /scripts/performance.sql
3. Run queries from /scripts/sample_queries.sql or write your own to see that database work
